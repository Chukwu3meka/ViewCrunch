Appproximately 3000 people die every year on the roads. We argue for the right road safety measures to tackle all the issues, including drunk driving, new drivers, speeders and so on. We all call for changes to the roads and vehicles, to make accident less likely to happen and to reduce injury when they do. Some simple tips to keep you calm and safe while on the road:

  • You should only stop if it is a real emergency and you have no other choice. If possible drive on and leave the motor way at the nerest exit
  • Break failure; don't turn off the ignition, keep pumping the break slightly
  • If you can't drive when its raining; you're not the first person, kindly move away from the traffic lane
  • Switch on your hazard warning lights and if its safe to place a warning triangle, do that at 45m behind the vehicle
  • Wear a reflective jacket if possible
  • Always and under every circumstance must you be on sit belt while in a moving car
  • If you feel at risk from another person, return to your vehicle, fasten your seat belt and lock all the doors. Don't run into uncompleted buildings or even the woods
  • If there's network signal call for help else call your local emergency number
